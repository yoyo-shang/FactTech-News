Custom Loop Water Cooling 101: A Beginner's Guide

Learn the fundamentals of liquid cooling to achieve maximum performance and silence in your PC build.

12 min read

The standard air coolers and even All-In-One (AIO) liquid coolers are great, but for the ultimate PC enthusiast, nothing compares to a custom water cooling loop. It offers the best thermal performance, the quietest operation, and unmatched visual aesthetics.

1. Understanding the Components

A custom loop consists of several key parts: the pump, the reservoir, the water blocks (for CPU and GPU), the radiators, and the tubing. Each component plays a vital role in moving heat away from your hardware efficiently.

2. Planning Your Loop

Before buying any parts, you must plan the order of your loop (the "routing"). The most common order is Reservoir -> Pump -> Blocks -> Radiators -> Reservoir. Planning your routing ensures you have enough fittings and the correct tubing lengths.

3. Leak Testing: The Most Important Step

Once your loop is assembled, you MUST leak test it before turning on your full system. Using an air pressure tester or a separate power supply for the pump allows you to find leaks safely without risking your expensive components.

Pro Tip: Always use distilled water with a biocide/inhibitor or a pre-mixed coolant. Standard tap water will cause corrosion and biological growth in your loop over time.
